About Thailand Sailing

Thailand's Andaman Sea coast offers one of the most visually spectacular sailing experiences on the planet. Towering limestone karsts draped in tropical vegetation rise from emerald-green waters, creating a seascape unlike anywhere else. Phang Nga Bay, the Phi Phi Islands, and the Similan archipelago are just a few of the iconic destinations that await. The warm, sheltered waters and gentle northeast monsoon breezes make this an accessible and rewarding cruising ground.

Beyond the jaw-dropping scenery, Thailand sailing offers exceptional value. Gourmet Thai cuisine is available at every anchorage, from floating restaurants to beachside cookshops. The diving and snorkelling are world-class, with vibrant coral reefs and marine life ranging from whale sharks to manta rays. Thai hospitality, known as the warmth of the Land of Smiles, extends to every marina and anchorage, making this a destination that combines natural wonder with genuine human connection.

Best Time to Sail

Thailand's sailing season aligns with the dry northeast monsoon.

ac_unit

Early Season (Nov - Dec)

The monsoon fades and the Andaman coast opens up. Seas calm, visibility improves, and temperatures hover around 28-30°C. An excellent time to beat the peak crowds.

wb_sunny

Peak Season (Jan - Feb)

The driest, sunniest months at 29-32°C. Calm seas, clear skies, and the best underwater visibility for diving and snorkelling. The most popular period for charters.

local_florist

Late Season (Mar - Apr)

Warm at 30-33°C with occasional afternoon showers. The sea remains calm and warm, crowds thin, and rates soften. A great time for a tropical escape before monsoon season.
